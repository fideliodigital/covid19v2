Impacto COVID19 en E-Commerce: América Latina

Abril 30, 2020 | Lectura 7 min


Hicimos este reporte para ayudar de manera gratuita a nuestros clientes, aliados y a la comunidad empresarial con una perspectiva y tendencias actualizadas del impacto de la pandemia Covid19 en el E-Commerce en América Latina. Partimos de la información de 115 millones de transacciones online procesadas anualmente en más de 22 mil comercios en Argentina, Brasil, Colombia, Chile, México, Perú y Panamá.

Industrias con mayor crecimiento

Tiendas especializadas

Top Subindustria: Miscellaneous and specialty retail stores (71%), Construction materials, not elsewhere classified (7.3%), Miscellaneous publishing and printing (3.3%), Misc. general merchandise (2.4%), Florists (2.2%). MaM_: 313.9%. AaA_: 88.5%. Participación_: 5.6%.



Bienes para el hogar

Top Subindustria: Miscellaneous home furnishing specialty stores (59.9%), Furniture, home furnishings, and equipment stores, except appliances (23.6%), Sewing, needle, fabric, and price goods stores (6.4%), Household appliance stores (4.1%), Home supply warehouse stores (2.8%). MaM_: 249.5%. AaA_: 220.5%. Participación_: 3.6%.

Moda y calzado

Top Subindustria: Miscellaneous apparel and accessory shops (41.5%), Mens and womens clothing stores (16.3%), Shoe stores (14.3%), Family clothing stores (10.2%), Womens ready-to-wear stores (6.4%). MaM_: 96.4%. AaA_: 206.1%. Participación_: 6.7%.

Top Subindustria: Colleges, junior colleges, universities, and professional schools (38.5%), Sporting goods stores (35.2%), Schools and educational services ( not elsewhere classified) (13.5%), Elementary and secondary schools (3.9%), Business and secretarial schools (3%). MaM_: 78.6%. AaA_: 44.8%. Participación_: 5.7%.

Comidas

Top Subindustria: Eating places and restaurants (42.2%), Fast food restaurants (11.9%), Grocery stores, supermarkets (11.5%), Misc. food stores and convenience stores and specialty markets (11.4%), Bakeries (7.4%). MaM_: 63.5%. AaA_: 260.8%. Participación_: 1.4%.
